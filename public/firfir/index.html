How to Cook Ethiopian Firfir

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups injera (Ethiopian flatbread)
  • 1 cup berbere sauce (Ethiopian spice mixture)
  • 1/2 cup clarified butter or oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Break the injera into small pieces and set aside.
  2. In a large pan or skillet, heat the clarified butter or oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Saut√© until the onion becomes translucent.
  4. Stir in the berbere sauce, salt, and black pepper. Cook for a couple of minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
  5. Add the broken injera pieces to the pan and mix well to coat them with the sauce.
  6. Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth and stir gently to combine. Reduce the heat to low.
  7. Cover the pan and simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the injera softens and absorbs the flavors.
  8. Remove from heat and let it sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  9. Garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley.
  10. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious Ethiopian Firfir!

Note: Firfir is often served as a breakfast dish in Ethiopia and is typically accompanied by yogurt or cheese.
